Worthington Steel - Mechanical Design Engineer Intern

During my time at Worthington Steel, I worked on mechanical design upgrades across the pickle line and storage yard to improve equipment performance and layout efficiency.

One of my first projects was reworking a squeegee roll stand based on an outdated AutoCAD design. The original assembly had stability issues and was set at the wrong height, so I redesigned the entire unit in SolidWorks â€” improving its structure, alignment, and accessibility. The final design was implemented directly on the line.

I also developed a new carry-over table design from scratch for the stitcher entry/exit, collaborating with maintenance and vendors to finalize it for production. The system improved material flow and simplified transitions between key sections of the line.

Outside the facility, I redesigned the coil storage layout to maximize space and accessibility. The updated configuration more than tripled coil capacity while improving retrieval flow and equipment access.

In total, I created over 40 parts and multiple assemblies in SolidWorks and AutoCAD, producing full documentation and collaborating with suppliers to support manufacturing and installation. I also managed drawing revisions across multiple systems to keep plant records accurate and up to date.
